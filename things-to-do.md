📅 1. Booking & Availability Engine (High Impact)
Right now users can chat and negotiate, but adding a formal booking flow would be huge.

Calendar Integration: Let freelancers set their available days/hours.
Booking Modal: A dedicated flow for clients to select a date, time, and service type.
Status Tracking: "Pending", "Accepted", "Completed", and "Cancelled" states for jobs.
💳 2. Payment Setup & Escrow (Business Critical)
To truly monetize the platform, handling transactions is key.

Local Integrations: Since this is focused on Lebanon, integrating local payment providers (like Whish Money, OMT, or Cash on Delivery options) alongside Stripe.
Milestones / Escrow: Holding the money securely until the client confirms the job is done to build trust on both sides.
🔔 3. Notification System (Engagement)
Socket.io is great for live chat, but users need to know when they miss something.

Email Notifications: Send an email when a user gets a new message, an offer, or a booking request while they are offline.
In-app Bell Icon: A notification center to track history of offers received, reviews posted, etc.
🛡️ 4. Trust & Safety (User Confidence)
In a service marketplace, trust is everything.

KYC / Identity Verification: An admin process to verify a freelancer's Lebanese ID or phone number, giving them a "Verified Blue Tick" 🔵.
Report System: Allowing users to report inappropriate messages or fake profiles.
🎯 5. "Post a Job" Feature (Reverse Marketplace)
Instead of just clients searching for freelancers, let clients post what they need.

Job Board: Clients post "I need a plumber in Achrafieh this Tuesday."
Bidding System: Freelancers can browse these open requests and submit their offers/quotes directly.
👑 6. Admin Panel
You'll need a way to manage the platform without touching the database directly.

Admin Dashboard: See total users, active bookings, and revenue.
Moderation Tools: Ability to ban users, delete inappropriate reviews, or approve portfolios.
Which of these sounds the most exciting or necessary for your vision right now? We could start by implementing the Booking System or building out the Notifications feature!

🌟 7. "Available Right Now" (Urgent Hiring Mode)
Leverage the availability flag for an "Uber-style" urgent hiring mode.
Toggle UI: Show only online freelancers ready to work immediately in the user's specific location.
Value: Instant matching for quick gigs without waiting for proposals.

💼 8. Micro-Workspaces (Advanced Chat)
Transform the chat thread into a full project workspace after an offer is accepted.
Features: Pinned shared to-do list, milestone tracker, and a dedicated tab for files.
Value: Keeps users on the platform instead of moving to Slack/Trello.

🤝 9. "Guilds" or Freelancer Teams
Allow freelancers to team up and bid on complex projects together.
Feature: A designer and developer can form a "Guild" to bid as a unit on the Reverse Marketplace.
Value: Bridges the gap between hiring solo (risky) and agencies (expensive).

🎥 10. Video Intros (TikTok of Portfolios)
Allow users to upload short video bios that auto-play on hover in the browse page.
Feature: 30-to-60-second video introductions.
Value: Humanizes freelancers and builds trust much faster than text bios.

🤖 11. AI-Assisted "Scope" Builder for Clients
Help clients write better job descriptions for the "Post a Job" feature.
Feature: Conversational UI asking simple questions to auto-generate a professional, well-scoped job post and suggest a price range.
Value: Eliminates client anxiety and provides high-quality job posts for freelancers.

🏅 12. Gamified Trust & Vouching System
Build a community-driven trust system beyond traditional 5-star reviews.
Feature: Freelancers can "vouch" for other freelancers' specific skills, boosting their trust score.
Value: Fosters a community feel and makes it harder to fake reputation.
